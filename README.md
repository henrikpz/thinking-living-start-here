You're here because someone shared this URL with you and told you it's the way to begin.

3. Claude will read this repository and walk you through the rest. You don't have to know what comes next — Claude does. Just answer Claude's questions when they come.

That's it. The whole setup (which takes 30–60 minutes including installing one app and creating a GitHub account if you don't have one) is driven by Claude. You sit there, answer Claude's questions, and at the end you have a working personal knowledge base that you and Claude build together going forward.

## What you'll have at the end

- A **private GitHub repository** under your own account, called `thinking-living`. It contains Markdown files that describe who you are, what matters to you, and the contexts of your life. Only you can see it.
- **Claude Code Desktop** installed on your computer, with permission to read and write the contents of that repository.
- A **Kanban board** on GitHub for tracking todos.
- An empty but **structured starting point** — folder for `identity/`, `relationships/`, `life-spheres/`, `events/`, etc. — that you fill in over time, mostly by talking to Claude rather than typing into files yourself.

## What "thinking-living" actually is

It's a way of thinking with Claude that goes beyond a single conversation. Most people interact with Claude as if every conversation is its own island — they re-explain themselves every time. **thinking-living changes that.** The Markdown files in the repository become Claude's persistent context: who you are, who's in your life, what you're working on, how you communicate. Each conversation builds on the last. Claude can draft messages in your voice, remember your preferences, surface things from weeks ago that are relevant now.

It's not a product. It's a personal practice. It works because the structure is **yours** — your repository, your data, on your computer, in plain readable Markdown that you can edit yourself anytime.

## What's safe / private

- Your `thinking-living` repository is **private** by default. No one but you sees its contents.
- You stay in control of your **GitHub account, Claude account, and your computer**. Claude operates inside the boundaries you set.
- Claude **never sends content outside your machine** without telling you first in plain language. If something is about to go on the internet (a posted message, an email, a search query), Claude says so before acting and waits for your "yes."
- The setup is reversible. You can delete the repository anytime; there's no lock-in.
